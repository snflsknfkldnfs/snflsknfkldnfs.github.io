üü° YELLOW LEVEL
PARTNER A - FRAGER
Your Mission: Get 3 Weekend Activities
Ask about 3 different things your partner did:
  • Activity 1: "What did you do on Saturday?"
  • Activity 2: "And what about Sunday?"
  • Activity 3: "Did you do anything else?"
Follow-up Questions (Important!):
  • "Where exactly?" (place details)
  • "Who was with you?" (people)
  • "How long did you stay?" (time)
  • "Why did you choose that?" (reasons)
Dialog Goal:
Keep asking until you know about 3 activities + places + people!
PARTNER B - STORY TELLER
Your Mission: Tell about 3 Weekend Activities
Activity 1: "On Saturday morning I went to... with... We..."
Activity 2: "In the afternoon I... at the... It was..."
Activity 3: "On Sunday I visited... and we..."
Include These Details:
  • WHERE: specific places (stadium, shopping centre, grandma's house)
  • WHO: people you were with (friends, family, team)
  • HOW: how it felt (exciting, boring, funny, great)
Bonus Points:
Add time details: "for 2 hours", "all morning", "until 6 o'clock"
Add reasons: "because I love football", "because it was sunny"

PARTNER A - INVESTIGATOR
Your Challenge: Deep Conversation
Don't just ask basic questions - dig deeper!
  • "What was the best part of your weekend?"
  • "If you could change one thing, what would it be?"
  • "Which activity do you want to do again?"
  • "What made it so special/boring/funny?"
Advanced Follow-ups:
  • "Compared to last weekend, how was this one?"
  • "Would you recommend it to me? Why?"
  • "What's your plan for next weekend?"
Goal:
Create a natural conversation that flows for 5+ minutes!
PARTNER B - STORYTELLER
Your Challenge: Rich Stories
Don't just list activities - tell engaging stories!
  • Start each story with setting: "You know what happened on Saturday?"
  • Add emotions: "I was so excited because..."
  • Include problems/surprises: "But then..."
  • End with reflection: "Looking back, I think..."
Story Elements to Include:
  • Sequence: First... then... after that... finally...
  • Contrast: Usually I... but this time...
  • Evaluation: The best/worst part was...
